🤲 Artisan Pledge & Direct Support Framework

This document outlines Selwa’s binding commitment to Bhutanese maker communities. It serves as a supplemental addendum to our Terms of Service and embodies our core promise: empower, preserve, and celebrate Bhutanese craftsmanship.

🇧🇹 Fair Compensation Guarantee

Selwa ensures that artisans receive between 75% – 85% of the final retail price (depending on product category). This is significantly above industry standards. We publish yearly impact metrics, including average incomes for each partner collective. We never negotiate unfair rates — all pricing agreements are co-designed with artisan groups.

🧵 Transparency & Storytelling

Every product page includes details about the artisan or village where it was made. Whenever possible, we feature the maker’s name, their craft lineage, and the cultural significance. We do not anonymize traditional crafts. Customers can also send digital "thank you notes" that we translate and pass to the artisans (only with your permission).

🛡️ Ethical Supply Chain

We operate zero child labor, zero forced labor, and safe working conditions. Regular on-site visits from Selwa representatives (in partnership with local NGOs) guarantee that workshops meet our ethical code. Third-party audits happen annually. Artisans can raise concerns anonymously through our grievance channel (support@selwa.bt).

🌿 Community Investment & Cultural Preservation

At least 5% of Selwa’s net profits are reinvested into artisan-led initiatives: weaving looms, natural dye workshops, and youth apprenticeship programs in Bhutan. By purchasing, you directly contribute to sustaining endangered crafts like yathra (yak wool weaving) and thagzo (traditional loom techniques).

📦 Special Conditions for Custom Orders

If you commission a bespoke artisan piece (e.g., handwoven kira, wooden mask), we will provide a transparent breakdown of materials + time + artisan fee. Due to handmade nature, production may take 6–12 weeks. You can request progress updates, and cancellation before weaving begins is eligible for refund minus 15% planning fee. After start, order is final but may be transferable.

📍 Dispute Resolution for Artisans

Artisans and collectives have direct mediation rights with Selwa management. Any payment dispute or quality claim will be addressed within 14 business days. We commit to translation services (Dzongkha/English) and fair arbitration.

🤝 Zero greenwashing, real impact

This pledge is legally binding and auditable. We partner with the Bhutan Chamber of Commerce & Industry to validate artisan payments. For each order, you receive a digital "impact receipt" showing the exact amount that goes to the maker community.
