Maker directory

Meet the hands behind every piece in the collection.

Each artisan on Selwa works within a living tradition passed down through generations. Their craft reflects the materials, rhythms, and cultural identity of the regions they call home.

Featured

Karma Choden

Craftsperson · Thimphu, Bhutan

Karma specialises in traditional Bhutanese bamboo weaving and craft production. She learned bangchung weaving from her mother and has since expanded her practice to include incense rolling and gift curation. Her work is rooted in everyday Bhutanese craft traditions rather than ceremonial production.

Tashi Dorji

Wood carver · Paro, Bhutan

Tashi trained under master carvers at the Zorig Chusum institute before returning to his family workshop in Paro. He produces carved wooden serving boards, decorative pieces, and souvenirs, finishing each with a traditional hand-rubbed lacquer technique.

Palang Woodwork Souvenirs

Dorji Wangchuk

Farmer and producer · Bumthang, Bhutan

Dorji runs a small organic farm in Bumthang, producing wildflower honey, buckwheat noodles, herbal teas, and wild-harvested cordyceps. His products are minimally processed to preserve the natural character of Bhutan's high-altitude ecosystem.

Honey Cordyceps Herbal tea

Kinley Pem

Herbalist · Thimphu, Bhutan

Kinley draws on Sowa Rigpa, the traditional Tibetan-Bhutanese system of medicine, to create botanical wellness products. She formulates herbal creams and essential oil sprays using plants sourced from trusted mountain suppliers across Bhutan.
